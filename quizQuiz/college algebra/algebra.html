This course is designed for students interested in Mathematics, Science, Engineering, Technology, and Education. This course is an in-depth exploration of algebra topics designed to ultimately prepare students for Calculus or further education courses. Topics covered include the following:

Section 1: Functions


A polynomial is an algebraic expression that is composed of terms. Each term in a polynomial consists of a coefficient multiplied by one or more variables raised to non-negative integer powers. The variables in a polynomial are often denoted by letters, such as x and y, and the coefficients are constants that multiply the variables.



Polynomials can be added, subtracted, multiplied, and divided, much like regular numbers. They are used to represent various mathematical relationships, such as describing the behavior of functions, solving equations, and approximating real-world phenomena. Polynomials are essential in calculus, where they help analyze functions and their behavior.




  • Degree: The degree of a polynomial is the highest power of the variable in any term.
  • Term: A term in a polynomial is a combination of a coefficient and a variable raised to a power.
  • Constant Term: A term with no variable is called a constant term.
  • Leading Coefficient: The coefficient of the term with the highest power of the variable is called the leading coefficient.
